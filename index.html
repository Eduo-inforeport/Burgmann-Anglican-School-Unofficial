School Front Office

Established in 1999

Thomas and Catherine Gribble travelled thousands of kilometres to settle on this same site. They used some of the local stone and mud to build their home in the late 1800s. You can see some of the remains of their farm behind the Valley Campus today. Its first principal was Dr George Browning who has served as principal for 23 years at 2 independent schools.



Forde Assembly Hall

Was also established in 1999(Most BAS buildings were completed in that same year)

This monumental building has been respected by students, many special arranged appearances have been set here. Every assembly, students acknowledge the Ngunnawal country.



Forde Canteen
Was also established in 1999

Many scrumptious delights have been sold here. This teaches students the way of money management and budgeting. Therefore being a luxury educational building as well as containing some excellent cooks!

School Achievments

Kayla Hardy
Kayla Hardy, student of Burgmann has joined the women's Australian national swimming team

We congratulate Burgmann Alumna, Kayla Hardy ('21) as she represents Australia in the World Swimming Championships as part of the record-breaking Australian women’s swim team, Australian Dolphins Swim Team Kayla was selected by the Australian Dolphins after winning three national titles at the Australian Short Course championships. We are thrilled when students and alumni can achieve their goals. Our High Performance program allows students to continue to complete their studies while training and striving to fulfil their talent in other areas.

National History Challenge
Year 12 student considered "Young Historian"

The National History Challenge is a research-based competition for students run by the History Teachers’ Association of Australia (HTAA). It gives students a chance to be an historian and rewards quality research. Burgmann Year 12 student, Elizabeth Spollard was announced as the ACT Young Historian for her essay applying the theme of Turning Points to the generational impact of the Tudors on England. She was joined by Junior School students Georgia Karle, Aisha Ismaili, Jade Norwood, Jamie Cadiz, Tessa Thirkettle, Saskia Leevers, and Tuyen Khuu, as winners in their respective year level categories. Casey Selfe, Olivia Bennett, and Summer Freer won the Museum Exhibit category. Burgmann won 5 of the 12 categories plus the State title. The joy of studying History is alive and well at Burgmann!

ACU Poetry Prize
John Foulcher, former teacher at Burgmann is awarded ACU Poetry Prize

We're very proud of the achievements of our staff and students. This #FlashbackFriday we're looking back to 2019 when Burgmann’s former Head of Senior School and author of the BAS History Book, John Foulcher, was awarded the coveted ACU Prize for Poetry. When asked what his poem ‘Revising Casuarinas’ is about, John explained “It’s a poem about ageing, and the solace we find in things and the things we do as we grow older. It’s about memory, love, affection and friendship.

What's New

  • 2022,New Principal Appointed: Leonie Harwood

    We are delighted to announce that the Burgmann Anglican School Board has appointed Ms Leonie Harwood as Principal from Term 1, 2022. Leonie’s appointment marks another milestone in our school’s history as both the third Principal and more importantly for a co-educational school, our first female Principal. Leonie is currently Deputy Principal and Head of Senior School at St John’s Grammar School, an Anglican ELC to year 12 school in the Adelaide Hills in South Australia. She is an inspiring, contemporary educator with a passion for academic excellence and ongoing learning for teaching staff. What shone brightly during the interview process was her warmth of personality and commitment to every child; she is committed to providing authentic, personalised learning experiences that give students an educational and life advantage. Leonie tells us: “I am excited and honoured to be appointed as the third Principal of Burgmann Anglican School. I am a strong believer in Christian education and look forward to joining Burgmann and leading a school community actively working to create a world where anything is possible for our young people grounded in faith.” We all look forward with great optimism to this next stage in the Burgmann story welcoming Leonie and her family to our Burgmann family next year!

  • COVID-19 Update

    A COVID-19 Burgmann Anglican School Update – Sunday 22 August 2021. The School is aware of new exposure site updates by ACT Health of various bus routes servicing Gungahlin. We understand you may have concerns about the status of school bus routes for our School. Burgmann Anglican School bus services are not currently listed as exposure sites. Should this situation change, we will advise our parents and staff via our official communication channels, the Portal and direct email. As always, we strongly encourage our community to follow the advice of ACT Health. ACT Health regularly updates the listing of exposure sites. If you or your family have been to any listed exposure locations at the dates and times specified, including any of the public transport options listed, you should monitor for symptoms, quarantine and/or get tested for COVID-19 as advised by ACT Health. If you are directed by ACT Health to get tested for COVID-19 or to isolate at any time, we urge our community to follow those directions. The best action we can all take is to stay locked down and limit our movements for this seven day period. If you feel unwell, get tested. If you are able to, get vaccinated.
